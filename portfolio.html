    Hypothetically, when one goes to rate a restaurant, they rate their experience with customer service. A way to prove that was to create a rate your server app. If the ratings match those of the resturant, then we would know we were correct. I created a rate your server app that would be brought to the customer at the table, along with their check. Dsiplayed on an IPad, the customer would just have to click through a few pages. The servers have already logged into the app so the customer would not have to scroll through numerous servers just to find theirs. I stylized my rate your server app off of the restaurant Calexico's.

    Brain notes. This app was made for educators ranging from pre-school, to university professors. Learning names isn't the easiest task to complete, especially when you have 25-100 kids in your class. This app was created so an educator can learn their student's names fast, which would in turn help the educator and student create a bond quicker. This app would be something purchased by a school, such as canvas or schoology. From there, the app is customizable according to preference. Two games are available to choose from to help remember student names. They are matching, and flashcards. If the app grows, so does its range of games, and customizations.
